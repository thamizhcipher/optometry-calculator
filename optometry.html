Retinoscopy Working Lens

Note: Provide your working distance (cm) from the patient's eye to the retinoscope while performing retinoscopy to calculate the compensating working lens

Instructions: Simply enter the working distance (cm), then click 'Calculate'

Formula:

  • Compensating lens for working distance = 1/D
  • D = Distance from patient's eye to retinoscope(cm)

Visual Acuity in 6 Notations

Instructions: Select any one line of visual acuity from any of the 6 dropdown menus; it will simultaneously display the corresponding visual acuity line for all other notations.

Select from dropdown
How to convert?

1. Snellen Meter to Feet = MAR x 20

2. Snellen Meter to MAR = Divide the Denominator by Numerator of Snellen Meter notation

3. LogMAR = log₁₀(MAR)

4. Snellen Meter to Decimal = Divide the Numerator by Denominator of Snellen Meter notation

5. Snellen Decimal to percentage = Decimal * 100

Steps for Simple Transposition:

  • Sum: Add the sphere and cylinder powers of the prescription to determine the new sphere power.
  • Sign: Change the sign of the cylinder.
  • Axis: Change the axis by 90 degrees. If the original axis is less than or equal to 90°, add 90° to the axis; if more than 90°, subtract 90°.

Snellen Letter Size

Note:The height and width of the individual Snellen letters remain the same
Instructions: Select the chart's distance and line of Snellen's chart from the dropdown menus, then click 'Calculate'







How to calculate?

    W = D x tanθ
  • W = Height or width
  • D = Denominator of the line
  • θ = 5 (min of arc)
  • tan5/60 ≈ 0.00145
