AC/A Ratio Calculator (Heterophoria)

Note: Provide the IPD, NFD, and phoria at near and distance to get the AC/A ratio.

Instructions: Enter the IPD, NFD, and phoria at near and distance, then click 'Calculate'.

Formula:

AC/A Ratio = IPD + (Hn - Hd) x NFD

  • IPD - Inter Pupillary Distance (cm)

  • Hn - Heterophoria at Near (PD)

  • Hd - Heterophoria at Distance (PD)

  • NFD - Near Fixation Distance (m)

AC/A Ratio Calculator(Gradient method)

Note:Give the deviations with and without the lens, and get the AC/A ratio

Instructions:Select the deviations in prism diopters from the dropdown, choose the lens used, then click 'Calculate'

Result:

Formula:

AC/A Ratio = (DL - DO) / p

  • DL - the Deviation with lens prism(D)
  • DO - Original Deviation without lens Prism(D)
  • p - Power of the lens used (D)

Sheard's Criterion

Note:Provide the Phoria and Compensating Fusional Vergence to calculate the prism
Instructions: Select the phoria and Compensating Fusional Vergence from the dropdown menus, then click 'Calculate'.

Formula:
  • P = 2/3(p) - 1/3 (CFV)
  • P - Prism(PD)
  • p - phoria(PD)
  • CFV - Compensating Fusional Vergence(PD)

Percival Criterion Calculator

Note:Provide the Greater lateral limit and Lesser lateral limit in prism diopters to calculate the prism.
Instructions: Select the Greater lateral limit and Lesser lateral limit from the drop-down menus, then click 'Calculate'..

Prism: 0.00(PD)

Formula:

    P = (1/3) * G - (2/3) * L
  • P = Prism to be prescribed
  • G = Greater of the two lateral limits(PD) (base-in or base-out)
  • L = Lesser of the two lateral limits(PD) (base-in or base-out)

Bielschowsky's Head Tilt Test

Note:Provide the hypertropia positions to identify the isolated muscle palsy
Instructions: Simply select the hypertropia in primary position, gaze, and head tilt, then click 'Submit Test'

Park's 3 Stpes
  • Note:By noting how the hypertropia changes with eye movements and head tilts, you can figure out which eye muscles might be causing the misalignment by the following steps.
  • Stpe 1: which eye is hypertropic in primary position?
  • Step 2: Whether the hypertropia increases on right or left gaze?
  • Step 3: Whether the hypertropia increases on right or left head tilt?
    • Hints
  • If all 3 steps are right - Left Inferior oblique
  • If all 3 steps are left - Right Inferior oblique
  • If 1st step only is right - Left Superior Rectus
  • If 1st step only is left - Right Superior Rectus
  • If 2nd step only is right - Left Superior Oblique
  • If 2nd step only is left - Right Superior Oblique
  • If 3rd step only is right - Left Inferior Rectus
  • If 3rd step only is left - Right Inferior Rectus

Amplitude of accommodation

Note: You can get Near Point of Accommodation ↔ Amplitude of Accommodation
Instructions: Provide either the Near Point of Accommodation or the Amplitude of Accommodation, then click the 'Calculate' button.
Make sure to erase the two inputs before the next calculation.

How to calculate ?

  • Amplitude of Accommodation(D): 100 / Near Point of Accommodation (cm)
  • Near Point of Accommodation(cm): 100 / Amplitude of Accommodation (D)

Hoffstetter's Amplitude Calculator

Note:Provide the Phoria and Compensating Fusional Vergence to calculate the prism
Instructions: Enter the age and click 'Calculate'



Hoffstetter's Formula
  • Minimum: 15 - (0.25 x Age)
  • Average: 18.5 - (0.30 x Age)
  • Maximum: 25 - (0.40 x Age)

Presbyopia Addition Calculator

Note:

Note: Provide the Reading Distance and Near Point of Accommodation to calculate the Near ADD
Instructions: Enter the Reading Distance and Near Point of Accommodation, then click 'Calculate'

Tip: If there's a Distance correction, simply add the Near ADD with the Sphere power to get the Near Prescription.

1/2 Amplitude reserved

Try Nearest Power as you required.

2/3 Amplitude reserved

Try Nearest Power as you required.

How to calculate ?
  • Keep 1/2 of Amplitude Reserve: Near Add = Working Distance (D) - 1/2 Amplitude of Accommodation(D).
  • Keep 2/3 of Amplitude Reserve: Near Add = Working Distance (D) - 2/3 Amplitude of Accommodation(D).
